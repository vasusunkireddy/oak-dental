Dental Care Tips for Pregnancy

1. Frequent Dental Check-ups

At least once in 2 months to avoid infections that can affect the fetus.

2. Brush and Floss

Brush twice daily and floss before going to bed.

3. Professional Teeth Cleaning

For removal of calculus deposits (scaling).

4. Avoid Self-Medication

Caution: Self-medication is dangerous!

5. Avoid Certain Drugs

Be extremely cautious on usage of drugs during pregnancy. Avoid tetracycline as they cause discoloration of your child's temporary & permanent teeth.

6. Be Aware of Risks

Pregnancy Gingivitis: Swollen red gums, bleeding while brushing.
Pregnancy Tumors: Benign growths in the gums, subside post-childbirth.
Premature Delivery: Bacterial infections can enter bloodstream, leading to premature delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is root canal treatment painful?

No, root canal treatments at OAK Dental Hospital are performed under local anesthesia, ensuring a pain-free experience. Our advanced techniques also minimize discomfort during recovery.

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime. Regular dental check-ups and good oral hygiene are key to their longevity.
